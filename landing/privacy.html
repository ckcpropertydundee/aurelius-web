Section 01

Who we are

Aurelius Property Management (“Aurelius”, “we”, “us”, “our”) is a property management platform based in Dundee, Scotland. We are the Data Controller for personal data processed through our web application and, when launched, our iOS application.

Contact for data matters: ckcpropertydundee@gmail.com

This policy is written to comply with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

Section 02

What this policy covers

This policy applies to all personal data we process in connection with the Aurelius platform, including:

  • Use of the Aurelius web application at aurelius-web-nine.vercel.app
  • Use of the Aurelius iOS application (when available)
  • Communications you send to us by email or through the platform
  • Payment and tenancy transactions processed on your behalf

It covers three categories of user: landlords, tenants, and contractors. Where data handling differs materially between landlords and tenants, we describe each separately.

Section 03

Data we collect

Landlords
  • Account: full name, email address, hashed password (managed by Supabase Auth)
  • Portfolio: property addresses, rental values, tenancy terms, property descriptions
  • Financial — Stripe Connect: to receive rent payments you onboard a Stripe Express account. Aurelius does not store raw bank account details; these are held by Stripe under their own privacy policy. We store your Stripe account ID, onboarding status, and payout enablement flags.
  • Management fee: your agreed management fee percentage, stored in your landlord profile
  • Payment history: rent payment records, management fee deductions, payout records, and associated Stripe payment intent IDs
  • Session & device: IP address, browser type, login timestamps, session tokens
Tenants
  • Account: full name, email address, hashed password (managed by Supabase Auth)
  • Contact: phone number (if provided)
  • Tenancy: property address, tenancy start and end dates, monthly rent amount, rent due date, tenancy status
  • Payment method: card payment methods are tokenised and stored by Stripe. We store your Stripe customer ID and a reference to your default payment method; we never see or store raw card numbers or CVVs.
  • Payment history: rent payment records, payment status, dates, amounts
  • Session & device: IP address, browser type, login timestamps, session tokens
Contractors
  • Account: full name, email address, hashed password
  • Business: company name, trade type, contact details
  • Job records: assigned work orders, completion status, communications logged within the platform

We do not collect special category data (as defined in UK GDPR Article 9) unless you voluntarily provide it. Please do not submit health, immigration status, or other sensitive data through the platform.

Section 05

Legal bases for processing

Under UK GDPR Article 6, we rely on the following legal bases:

  • Contract Processing is necessary to provide the service you have contracted for — account creation, tenancy management, and rent collection. This is the primary basis for most processing of landlord and tenant data.
  • Legal Obligation We must retain financial records for a minimum of six years under UK tax and company law. We may also need to process data to comply with requests from HMRC or other authorities.
  • Legitimate Interests Fraud detection, platform security, and service improvement, where these interests are not overridden by your rights. We conduct a balancing test before relying on this basis.
  • Consent For any optional marketing communications. You may withdraw consent at any time without affecting other processing.
Section 06

Third-party processors

We engage the following processors under written Data Processing Agreements. They act only on our instructions and may not use your data for their own purposes.

Supabase
Database · Authentication · Storage

Our platform database, authentication service, and file storage run on Supabase infrastructure hosted in eu-west-1 (Ireland) — within the EEA, which has UK adequacy status. Supabase processes your account data, tenancy records, and payment references on our behalf.

Stripe
Payment Processing · Connect Accounts

Stripe Payments Europe, Ltd processes all payment card data and manages landlord Express Connect accounts. Stripe is a regulated payment institution. We do not receive or store raw card or bank account numbers — only Stripe-issued references and status fields.

Vercel
Web Hosting · Edge Network

Our web application is hosted on Vercel’s global edge network. Vercel may process request-level data (IP addresses, request headers) as part of serving the application and providing DDoS protection. Standard Contractual Clauses are in place for any transfers outside the UK/EEA.

Apple (future)
iOS App Distribution

When the iOS application launches, it will be distributed via the Apple App Store. Apple processes data required for app installation and in-app purchase flows under Apple’s own privacy policy. Aurelius will update this section when the iOS app is live.

We do not sell your personal data to any third party. We do not use your data for behavioural advertising.

Section 07

Data retention

Data type Retention period Reason
Active account data Duration of account Contract performance
Payment & financial records 7 years from transaction date UK tax law (HMRC requirement)
Account data after deletion request 30 days, then permanently deleted Grace period for accidental deletion
Session & security logs 90 days Fraud investigation & security
Tenancy records post-tenancy 3 years from tenancy end Legitimate interest; dispute resolution

After the applicable retention period, data is deleted or irreversibly anonymised. You may request earlier deletion under your right to erasure (Section 10), subject to the overriding financial record requirements above.

Section 08

International transfers

Our primary data storage is in Ireland (eu-west-1), which falls within the EEA. The UK has granted adequacy status to the EEA, meaning transfers between the UK and Ireland do not require additional safeguards.

Vercel operates a global edge network that may route requests through servers outside the UK and EEA. Vercel has implemented Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) for such transfers. For data that never leaves Supabase’s eu-west-1 region (account data, tenancy records, payment references), no international transfer occurs.

Stripe Payments Europe, Ltd is an Irish-incorporated entity. Card processing may involve Stripe’s global infrastructure, which is covered by Stripe’s own transfer mechanisms and binding corporate rules.

Section 09

Cookies & local storage

Aurelius does not use third-party advertising or tracking cookies. Our use of browser storage is limited to what is necessary to operate the platform:

  • Session storage (localStorage): Supabase JS stores your authentication session token in localStorage to keep you logged in between page loads. This token is used solely to authenticate requests to our backend. It is cleared when you log out.
  • Stripe.js: Stripe’s client-side library may set cookies for fraud detection purposes (e.g. device fingerprinting for card authentication). This is a requirement of secure card processing under PSD2. See Stripe’s cookie policy for details.

We do not use Google Analytics, Meta Pixel, or any behavioural tracking tools. If this changes, we will update this policy and, where required, request your consent.

Section 10

Your rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the following rights in relation to your personal data. To exercise any of them, email us at ckcpropertydundee@gmail.com. We will respond within one calendar month.

  • Right of Access You can request a copy of all personal data we hold about you, along with information about how it is processed (a “Subject Access Request”).
  • Right to Rectification If your data is inaccurate or incomplete, you can ask us to correct it. Much account data can be updated directly in your platform settings.
  • Right to Erasure You can ask us to delete your personal data. We will comply unless we have a legal obligation to retain it (e.g. financial records required by HMRC).
  • Right to Restriction You can ask us to restrict processing of your data in certain circumstances — for example, while a dispute about accuracy is resolved.
  • Right to Portability Where processing is based on contract or consent and carried out by automated means, you can request your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Right to Object You can object to processing based on legitimate interests. We will stop unless we can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds that override your interests.
  • Automated Decisions We do not make solely automated decisions that produce legal or similarly significant effects. Role assignment, access control, and rent calculations involve human oversight.
